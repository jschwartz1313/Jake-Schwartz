Mission Statement

I got into environmental science because I wanted to work on consequential problems, and energy quickly became the area that felt most important. What drew me toward information science was the ability to turn complex datasets into analysis that organizations can actually use. I'm most interested in work that combines technical analysis with real-world decision-making, whether that's geospatial screening for energy projects, evaluating new technologies, or understanding market and policy dynamics.

Energy and clean tech are inherently interdisciplinary, and the most important questions usually sit at the intersection of finance, economics, infrastructure, regulation, and policy. Understanding how regulation affects project economics, how transmission constraints affect renewables deployment, or how storage and DER change grid operations makes me a stronger analyst. I spend a lot of time listening to podcasts because they help me stay current across that broader landscape. Some of my favorites are Odd Lots, Energy Empire, Energy Policy Now, and Up First.

I'm also someone who leans into new tools rather than avoiding them. I regularly use ChatGPT, Claude, Claude Code, and Codex to automate workflows, accelerate research, structure analysis, and handle repetitive tasks more efficiently. Used well, these tools make me far more productive and let me spend more time on higher-value thinking, synthesis, and decision support.
